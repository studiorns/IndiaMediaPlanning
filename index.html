Market Insights

Key Takeaway

The India market shows a strong year-round performance with peaks in Q4. November and December represent the highest hotel guest months, with September and October showing the strongest travel query activity.

Seasonal Pattern

The India market demonstrates consistent performance throughout the year with notable peaks in Q4. Hotel guests peak in November (43,636) and December (43,813), while travel queries show the strongest performance in September (180.19) and October (149.04). The market maintains strong performance even during traditionally slower periods, indicating year-round travel interest.

Seasonal Distribution

  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 27% of annual travel queries
  • Spring (Mar-May): 24% of annual travel queries
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 22% of annual travel queries
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): 27% of annual travel queries

Peak Performance

  • Top Travel Query Months: September (367.74), October (253.05)
  • Top Hotel Guest Months: December (43,813), November (43,636)
  • Top Flight Search Months: September (29,299), October (23,876)
Strategic Implication

Marketing resources should be distributed throughout the year with increased focus on Q3 campaigns to capture the Q4 travel peak, considering the 44-day booking window. The consistent year-round performance suggests opportunities for growth in traditionally slower periods.

Focus on maintaining the high intent-to-consideration ratio while addressing key barriers to visit, particularly negative word-of-mouth experiences and perceptions about weather. The high awareness and consideration metrics suggest shifting focus to conversion rather than awareness-building.

Key Takeaway

The India market requires a targeted strategy focused on addressing negative word-of-mouth perceptions, weather concerns, and repeat visitation opportunities, while capitalizing on the strong year-round travel interest and high conversion metrics.

Year-Round Engagement Strategy

Distribute marketing resources throughout the year with a 30% increase in Q3 campaigns to capture the Q4 travel peak. Develop targeted campaigns for traditionally slower periods to capitalize on the consistent year-round interest.

Create season-specific content highlighting unique experiences available in Abu Dhabi during different times of the year, with special emphasis on countering weather-related concerns.

Perception Enhancement

Address the "bad experiences" barrier (55%, +15pp) through a dedicated testimonial campaign featuring positive experiences from Indian travelers. Develop content that directly counters weather-related concerns (32%, -6pp) by highlighting indoor activities and climate-controlled attractions.

Increase user-generated content from Indian travelers by 40% across all channels to counter negative word-of-mouth perceptions.

Repeat Visitation Focus

Develop a "Return to Abu Dhabi" campaign targeting the 35% of non-considerers who have already visited, highlighting new attractions and experiences opened since 2022.

Create exclusive packages and loyalty benefits for repeat visitors from India, with promotional push beginning 45-60 days before peak travel periods (aligned with the 44-day booking window).
